It's good to know what to stuff your pockets with...

I started smoking when a full pack would cost 40 paisa only. I have smoked for 50 years of my life until my heart gave up. The extreme was 62 cigarettes in one day when I was super stressed due to an accidental death in my family. Smoking was my go - to solution for stress, tension and grief. It was introduced by a colleague who would religiously offer 1 every night until I got addicted. Eventually over the years I reached around 40 to 45 each day. I have been pressurised by my wife several times to quit smoking but I would always find creative excuses. Once I made a casual promise that if smoking gets me to a hospital I'll quit. Finally, a heart attack got me to quit . Post my heart surgery I came home breathing easy but my body craved for nicotine. I tricked it by taking candies that would keep my mouth from going dry! Today my pocket is always filled with candies. You know, once after a promise to my granddaughter , I had given up smoking. I controlled my urge for three whole months until a gentleman at the park offered me a smoke. I went back to buying cartons again.

It's very important not to take that one puff once you have quit smoking. I am committed to not return to smoking again. When you quit, you must be strong to keep up the promise.

Sibendranath Roy

Recently one of my friends gifted me a carton of cigarettes not knowing that I had quit. I'm glad that I could give it away and tuck candies in my pocket instead. It's good to know what to stuff your pockets with.
