Don't let your future go up in smoke...

The best day of my life - I successfully quit smoking. For many people, the act of quitting smoking happens multiple times in life - once, twice, thrice and perhaps more. From that perspective, I consider myself lucky that I had more mental strength because I was able to pull it through in one clean strike. But it was not easy! For the first 2-3 weeks, there was immense pressure both mentally & physically, something that not only I had to suffer but also my family and friends.

. I had finally figured out how to quit in my own way. Cigarette is one such thing that influences your mood in surprising manners; its so hot outside - lets smoke one; it’s so cold outside - lets smoke one. I would wake up and start searching for the pack subconsciously - I explicitly stopped the act. Immediately after lunch or dinner, I used to crave for a puff of smoke - again, I forcefully stopped the urge to light up a cigarette.

In order to challenge myself, I started telling others to incite me in the most rude and negative way that I won’t be able to quit. The more they incited me - the more determined I was becoming to quit, to the extent that I turned it into an ego issue. So, whenever I had the cravings, I used to remember those faces challenging me, and my urge would go away miraculously. It has been four years since I quit smoking - today even if I stand in a cigarette factory, I won't smoke! I never used to believe that "cigarette smoking is injurious to health".

Give up! It would be better for yourself and your dear ones. At the end of it, it is you who CAN control your life. DON'T let your addiction make that move.

Saswata Chaterjee

But after I quit, my mind and my body have gotten better, I feel fresher and more energetic throughout the day. As a matter of fact, cigarette makes an entry into our lives in a very naive and innocent manner and at a very young age. It starts with one cigarette - one because, in you school/college days, you can barely afford for that single one. By chance if you have someone in your family is a smoker, then you'd perhaps start stealing one/two from the packet, and before you know it, this one-two-three turns into 20-30-40-50 a day.

You won't even realize! And when we realize, coming out of that addiction becomes an almost impossible task. I urge everyone to not even think of taking that route! People who are already addicted - Give up! It would be better for yourself and your dear ones. At the end of it, it is you who CAN control your life. DON'T let your addiction make that move :)
