The Day you Decide is the Day you Achive....!!!

Vijay Bhat talks about how he quit smoking after being diagnosed with cancer a few years ago.
I started smoking long ago in 1977, shortly after enrolling in College after high school, and thereafter I was a regular smoker for about 5 years. Why did I begin smoking? Because all of my close friends smoked, and we thought it was cool. Thankfully, most young people no longer find smoking fashionable or sustainable. I always knew smoking was bad for me because I was an active sportsman engaged in a lot of physical activity.
A significant day of my life was August 2nd, 1982 on which I quit smoking and I'm proud to say that I haven't touched a cigarette since. It took a small amount of effort on my part, and all credit goes to my friends; there were a few people who tried to influence me to resume smoking, but the great majority of my friends prevented me from taking up cigarettes.
I firmly believe in "Small disciplines repeated with consistency every day lead to great achievements gained slowly over time." and this only motivated me always for not getting a cigarette in my hand....
My quitting journey began with a terrible cough, so I decided not to smoke for two days. After successfully finishing these two days, I didn't smoke for another week, and after a successful week, I gave up for a month. And so I looked at it for a lifetime, and it became simple for me to give up. I gradually regained my self-confidence, from days to week and week to year. And i think that's the best way to Quit. Similarly, it was incredibly difficult for my friend, so I have no illusions and believe you really need that internal switch to declare, "I'm giving up."
I can tell you that it is really essential to take care of your life because it is extremely valuable and your life is in your hands. So from my personal experience I can say “Healthy citizens are the greatest asset any country can have.”So, let's make India a healthier place.

Let's make India a healthier place..!

Vijay Bhat
