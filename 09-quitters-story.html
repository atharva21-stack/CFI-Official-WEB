Candies for Cigars..

I started smoking when a full pack would cost 40 paisa only. I have smoked for 50 years of my life until my heart gave up. The extreme was 62 cigarettes in one day when I was super stressed due to an accidental death in my family. Smoking was my go - to solution for stress, tension and grief. It was introduced by a colleague who would religiously offer 1 every night until I got addicted. Eventually over the years I reached around 40 to 45 each day.

I have been pressurized by my wife several times to quit smoking but I would always find creative excuses. Once I made a casual promise that if smoking gets me to a hospital I'll quit. Finally, heart attack got me to quit. When I was admitted to the hospital, my wife recovered 60 to 70 hidden packs of cigarettes in my cupboard. I used to always shop for cigarettes by the carton!

Post my heart surgery I came home breathing easy but my body craved for nicotine. I tricked it by taking candies that would keep my mouth from going dry! Today my pocket is always filled with candies.

Once you quit, you quit, there is no going back.

Dr.Sibendranath Roy

You know, once after a promise to my granddaughter I had given up smoking. I controlled my urge for three whole months until a gentleman at the park offered me a smoke. I went back to buying cartons again. It's very important not to take that one puff once you have quit smoking. I am committed to not return to smoking again. When you quit, you must be strong to keep up the promise.

Recently one of my friends gifted me a carton of cigarettes not knowing that I had quit. I'm glad that I could give it away and tuck candies in my pocket instead. And, now i can say that I am a successful tobacco quitter!
