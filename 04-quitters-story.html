Quit smoking before smoking quits you

Back in the days of 2003, I was on set, playing the role of Byomkesh Bakshi. This character was very chilled out and used to smoking cigarettes. I didn’t even know how to hold cigarettes in those days. I was completely a non-addict. Even if I was just asked to act like a smoker, I couldn’t do it. Then one person from our sound team trained me how to hold a cigarette.

This was the first time I had ever held a cigarette in my hand. He also taught me how to take a puff and release the smoke. I started liking and getting into the character day by day. I used to practice between takes, so on the final take, it looked authentic. This is where it all began.

Whenever I was questioned about the smoking addiction, I always had an excuse: "It's just for my role." I wasn’t guilty at all when I said this. This psychology started growing in my mind. I never realized I was getting trapped in the most harmful addiction out there, Even after Byomkesh Bakshi, I started searching for roles which allowed me to smoke openly for the style statement.

the best time to quit smkoing was the day you started, the second best time to quit is today.

Sudip Mukherjee

There was no addiction, it was just a casual smoking habit that started showing serious effects on my health. I was very fond of sport and fitness. I used to jog, workout, and practice yoga a lot. I was a national-level athlete as well. As smoking grew on me, I started to realize my body was not the same any more. I used to get very tired and it had had many adverse effects on my health

I realized this is serious. Why do I even need this? I decided to quit the same day. I used to have puffed rice and peanuts just to keep my mind diverted and not to think about smoking. It just helped to keep my mouth engaged. It was extremely tough in the prior days, but I made it! I’m finally back in "Sudip Mukherjee’s" role now! Cheers!
