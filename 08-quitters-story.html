Choice is yours, don't be too late

Unlike others, mine is slightly different from the ordinary textbook stories of smoking wherein people get introduced to smoking at an early age and get hooked on to the style. My story is very peculiar, for that matter. I was in Hyderabad at Ramoji Film City with Mr. Nitish Roy (the renowned Art Director). The site was like a Jungle, there was no one. It was a brutal lonely life out there. So, there I picked up smoking - out of loneliness and way to pass time!

Smokers generally have a typical body odor. Once I started smoking regularly, how could I be spared of that odor. So, I stopped going close to any woman, out of the fear that they would sniff that bad smell. When I used to meet my lady friends, I suddenly stopped hugging them. It was very unnatural of me, and they used to feel very surprised as if Rabi is not warm anymore! This guilt use to play within my mind very very strongly.

My mother used it to treat tooth sensitivity, gum infections, insect bites, leech bites, wounds, and other maladies. Duma (Tobacco) is a type of leaf. We clean it thoroughly before rubbing our teeth to relieve sensitivity. We apply it to wounds, cuts, and bites and cover it with a bandage. People still use duma (tobacco) to treat toothaches, infections, and wounds.

Now, it has come to a stage that if someone enters into a room after smoking, I go out of the room. For a test, I asked my son that I am going to smoke one cigarette. I would have dragged two puffs - and I felt so so bad that I threw away the cigarette.

Talking about money - I was burning money in stashes. The young kids would not understand the financial implications of such a bad habit. I used to buy cigarettes at a rate of Rs 320/packet, consume two packets a day. One day, I did the math and discovered that I was spending Rs 20,000/month on cigarettes!!

Quitting on smoking cigarette has given me a lot of courage and confidence. As a soul, I would feel guilty every single day. Now I am a free soul - both literally and figuratively!

Rabiranjan Maitra
