All you need is strong willpower..

I had never been so embarrassed before! It was December, 2012 and we were visiting Kanha National Park with a few friends and colleagues. We were lodged at the Baghira Log House, deep in the forest. I didn't smoke all day but gave up in the evening.

sneaked into the common open toilet and took a lungful! And what followed was utter chaos - monkeys, panicked and run amok creating complete pandemonium with the lodge manager and all others rushing out. That half an hour of humiliation and embarrassment in front of all my friends was enough for me to give up smoking. Since then I started avoiding cigarettes consciously.

. I tried alternatives but they did not work for me. When friends offered, I used to pocket it but would not smoke. I didn't officially announce that I had quit smoking until I was sure that I will not go back to it. Off and on I would crave for cigarettes if someone smoked around me but did not take a single puff.

It is not difficult at all. What matters is your willpower and desire to quit. So if you have to quit… Quit Now!

Dr. Rajarshi Roy

Smoking was a habit which I had picked up in college eighteen years ago. Smoking back then was looked upon as a symbol of having an attitude, being intellectual and superior. As medical students we weren't as conscious since we weren't practicing doctors.

Because as doctors we deal with the harms caused by smoking. To all young people I would like say - if there’s anything in your life that you want to quit, quit smoking first. It is not difficult at all. What matters is your willpower and desire to quit. So if you have to quit… Quit Now!
