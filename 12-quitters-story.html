Quit Now! Say "I will!" and then stick to it.That's the only way to do it.

I had never been so embarrassed before! It was December, 2012 and we were visiting Kanha National Park with a few friends and colleagues. We were lodged at the Baghira Log House, deep in the forest. I didn't smoke all day but gave up in the evening. I quietly sneaked into the common open toilet and took a lungful! And what followed was utter chaos - monkeys panicked and ran amok creating complete pandemonium with the lodge manager and all others rushing out. That half an hour of humiliation and embarrassment in front of all my friends was enough for me to give up smoking.
I've been conscientiously avoiding cigarettes since then. Recommendations ,Alternative methods were tried, but they didn't really work for me. When my friends offered, I used to pocket it but would not smoke. I didn't officially declare that I had quit smoking until I was certain that I'll never return to it. Off and on I would crave for cigarettes if someone smoked around me but did not take a single puff.
“To be of good quality, you have to excuse yourself from the presence of shallow and callow minded individuals.” Smoking was a habit I developed in college eighteen years ago. Having an attitude, being intellectual, and being superior were all associated with smoking back then. We weren't as concerned as medical students because we weren't practicing doctors. Because as doctors, we deal with the consequences of smoking

To be of good quality, you have to excuse yourself from the presence of shallow and callow minded individuals.

Michael Bassey Johnson

To all young people I would like to say - if there’s anything in your life that you want to quit, quit smoking first. It is not difficult at all. What matters is your willpower and desire to quit. So if you have to quit… Quit Now! Say "I will!" and then stick to it.That's the only way to do it.
