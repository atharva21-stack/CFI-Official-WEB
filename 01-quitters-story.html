Addiction of smoking to addiction of running

The goal in my mind to be a role model for my children made me quit smoking! It is quite difficult to be away from an addiction you are indulged into from many years. I went through the same indeed. My mind used to get diverted at every point of time. I dealt with stress, doubt and had my share of hurdles.

In the period of pandemic, when each and every human was like a frozen ice at home, had to go through work from home schedules; this bad habit of smoking took over me. It felt like I am going through a never-ending cycle and badly wanted to end this cycle as soon as I can.

I introspected and had a positive talk to myself each and every day! I just wanted to get out of this and made a way to deal with this. It was to throughly channelize my energy in the correct direction. A simple looking decision but an effective one - I started Hiking! Climbing the highest mountain in california felt like a good move which build up my confidence. I even ran and achieved the max distance and participated in marathons and won indeed! But, herecomes the fun part. I indulged myself in these activities to channelize my energy in the correct way. Unfortunately, it didn't happen. I used to celebrate each and every win through smoking ciggarettes. Felt crap again!

"You can do anything, just start concentrating and channelizing your energy into something."

Birendra Singh, IT Professional

My 39th birthday was a turning point in my life. I made a final decision. The decision to quit smoking - Once and for ALL! I planned a full-fledged plan, trained myself for a full marathon for over 16 weeks. With all the dedication, grit and confidence - I won! I won not only in the marathon but also in the decision I made for myself for a lifetime. And that day, I celebrated again. BUT, this time not with a puff.
