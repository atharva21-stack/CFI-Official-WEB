Got over the routine of chai, samosa and a smoke!

When I was at my lowest state in my college life, a junior got me into smoking. He said "be happy and live your life" so I was taken to a so-called gang where I found life with others who bonded over chai, samosa and a smoke. It became a regular routine while I was in college. Peer influence was so strong that nothing else mattered to me until one day, I was clicked with the stick and the image was sent to my then girlfriend. You can imagine what followed!!

I started smoking in college and gave up while in college. I was lethargic, having issues with playing football, with climbing stairs and was not myself. I sensed smoking was doing something to me. I distanced myself from the smoker friends and would not keep the sticks with me to avoid any urge to smoke. It was easier to give up since I was not quite an addict by then.

Make a decision, follow it and lead a smoke-free and positive Life!

Anirban Banerjee

Cigarettes are easier to be hooked on to. You can get it everywhere, walk with it, get company just about anywhere. In fact around my college, I found agents giving free cigarette samples, conducting survey on our smoking choices and what not! I believe that if you have just started smoking then this is the best time to quit. I have been through it.
