If you’re thinking about smoking think again

The scariest day of my life was 7th of February last year. A usual day turned a nightmare when I spitted fresh blood over and over while brushing my teeth. I was paralyzed with fear! I knew it was because of smoking! I got signals before which I ignored. Out of that fear I promised myself that I'm never going to touch another cigarette in my life. I didn't since and saved my voice. Quitting is about that one moment when you make up your mind.

I didn't start smoking in school as one would think but I was 32. An altercation with my then girlfriend got me hooked to cigarettes. It turned into a serious addiction at work where everybody around was smoking. Every meeting, every site visit, every casual discussion was over packs of cigarette. I used to buy loose cigarettes and have it while working at home late night. This was my routine until one day, my voice cracked. Out of nowhere I noticed I couldn't talk at a stretch. Everyone knows, I talk a lot.

would break when I had cold water or sang a few songs for pleasure.Then, the 7th happened when I rushed to my doctor who diagnosed my problem quickly. I somehow saved my voice box. That fear of losing my voice gave a lesson for life and I never picked a cigarette again. My experience has scared a few of my colleagues who have quit smoking since.

I can tell you from my experience that quitting is a personal choice and the only thing that works is determination. Today I am a health advocate and never allow anyone to smoke near me. I don't have a craving today and the very smell of tobacco irritates me. I truly would urge everyone to give up smoking - it's really not worth!

" It takes determination to see a dream come to pass. The question is not will you start, but will you finish.”

Subrata Singha, Construction Professional
