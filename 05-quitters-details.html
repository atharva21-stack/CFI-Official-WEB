  • Subrata Singha
  • 25 February 2021

I managed to quit smoking one day.

I managed to quit smoking one day and was doing well for three days until a colleague offered me one stick. Tobacco beat me! After a few years, I finally quit smoking.

Since I first started smoking, I was in Class X. I actually remember sneaking away to a hidden corner near my school to smoke with friends. Cigarettes were once lauded for their ability to help you think more critically. I used to smoke heavily before a difficult examination in college! I was working at a pack every day by the time I started my first job.

I once traveled to the United States for work. I was staying in the town of Bridgeport. . It was extremely difficult to obtain a cigarette over there. I'd have to drive seven miles to a gas station, where they were available at an exorbitant price. Every time I needed to smoke, I had to ask a coworker to drive me to the shop, which was an embarrassing inconvenience. That was the first time I realized I should quit smoking. Well, it wasn't that simple; I would patiently await the first opportunity for a cigarette break..

I actually tried several times until my wife made me promise. I tried several tricks, such as constantly reminding myself I wasn't going to give up, but delaying, consulting the internet, and putting rubber bands on cigarette packs, but nothing worked. Until, in the year 2000, I made a final decision and told myself that I will not be driven by tobacco.” It takes determination to see a dream come to pass. The question is not will you start, but will you finish”. And I had finally given up smoking. I felt fantastic.

" It takes determination to see a dream come to pass. The question is not will you start, but will you finish.”

Subrata Singha, Construction Professional

Although I am impressed to see relatively few people smoking today, I do notice that the smoking trend is shifting from men to women. I see spiffy free samples distributed to young lady professionals in the cha-er theks (local tea shops) around my office by women marketing cigarettes. Tobacco makes no distinctions between genders and harms everyone equally. Tobacco laws are becoming more stringent, with gruesome images on the packs. Most teenagers are unaware of how quickly nicotine can turn into an addiction. I know it is best not to take it up at all now that I have been through the entire journey.“It is in your moments of decision that your destiny is shaped.”
